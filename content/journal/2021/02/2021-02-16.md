my dream ended in a meditation chamber that looked like a foyer or waiting room. a row of marble benches were built into the wall and at the end of it a monk sat with a handchime on an elevated platform. I had followed sherlock and peter, those taiwanese university chinese major rogues, into this room, away from the crowd that we were with earlier, and we found seats on the meditation mats on the marble bench. my uncle who had died of cancer in real life was already there, along with his younger son who was crawling on his lap and making noises. we sat there and tried to meditate. peter and sherlock were draped in elaborate silk scarves. in my hands somehow i had a magazine, and i was drawn into reading it — there was a photo essay about some ancient megaliths deep in the forests of greece. before, the vast fires that had spread throughout the land had created vast orbs of heat that felled the trees but left them intact in a circular pattern, such that the trunks pointed outward, and the ancient greeks had constructed stone ramps to support them so they’d point upward at an angle. now after the changes in climate the whole forest would be reduced to cinders. the pages contained page after page of these circular marble ramps glistening in the sun. meanwhile the buddhist monk was still chanting with his handchime, and my cousin was still mewling.
