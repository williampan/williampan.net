From Maazin: 
> I woke up feeling rested, with fewer mosquito bites than usual - I’ve rarely been in the city of my birth during my birthday. From a nicotine withdrawn wake up, I walked downstairs to have uthhapam and sambar with my grandfather. I read a few chapters of Gibran, looking up every few pages at no one in particular. I thought of sending a photo of a chapter “I particularly enjoyed” but the idea of the performance preempted a sour taste in my mind. I took an earlier than anticipated drive to Buhari towers, following which I went to inspect a jeep my cousin was interested in buying - an orange GYPSY KING. I sat in the back while we test drove, wondering if I’d be recognised on the streets. The hot winter afternoon after a year away made the streets feel liminal. It’s the only city I take naps in, so I took the first of many. I woke up to go find an iced americano, wondering if I actually enjoyed the drink or drank it as an archetype of something I’m not sure I would like to be categorised as. “Are you a black coffee drinker?” asked my newfound barista friend to my cafe companion, “he used to live in Melbourne!” I retorted without any intention in particular. The joe was good, and conveniently located - small joys really.
