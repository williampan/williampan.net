In my dream, I wandered through a landscape that reminded me of an M. John Harrison obsession. Felt like the hills at the back of my neighborhood but covered with eucalyptus trees and litter and trails cut across it at strange angles. Saw a few deer. There were people with masks. Walked along an endless flat dry riverbed after that, the sun bright against the stones, people dressed shoddily approaching in clusters. Felt fear of catching the plague. After that I was in a building that is what I imagine Kowloon was like before it was torn down, small dark rooms separated by sheets of transparent flexible vinyl.
