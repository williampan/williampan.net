We began the night as usual: aimless. But the radio was tuned to KCSB, and there was a girl with an exaggerated Californian accent hosting—so we decided to drive to the university and glimpse the radio room from afar. We parked in Isla Vista, walked past the row of fraternities and sororities, crossed into university territory. We scanned the usual landmarks—Storke Tower, the library—walked the second level of the Brutalist performing arts building next to the library, approached the hill by the alumni hall with the fencegrown bougainvillea. Then we followed the road to Henley Gate, took photos in front of the colorshifting diode arrays. Henry scaled the inland end and approached the center but was too much of a coward to walk on the steel that bridged the void. We then took the long way round back to the car, following the vast array of physics and engineering buildings by the coast, stopping at the firebeacon.
