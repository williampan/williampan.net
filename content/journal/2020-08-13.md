In last night's dream I was convinced I was in Texas, but it looked more like New England---dead winter, deep snow. I was staying in a room with a line of beds, as if at an orphanage. I spent the day at a school campus, and when I came back the entire room was cleared out and a bomb squad had set up some elaborate scaffolding in the rafters in order to collect evidence without touching the floor. Apparently someone's legs had been blown off---a girl named Carina. Later, I was on a tablet, playing some video game, perhaps to find relief from the events of the day. It was a primitive little game---I was to select a team of characters and weapons, and see if they could face off the foes that would come. All the characters were crudely illustrated, as if I were playing with paper cutouts from an early picture book. There was a round of dragons, then of anthropomorphic eagles with bows and arrows and torpedoes, then the game started playing itself, with a nutcracker that ate apples several times faster than the competition.

The Lake Fire burned 11,000 acres in the hills east of Santa Clarita. Its ash blew downtown. After work we got Thai food from the place on Camino Real and ate it on the dunes at Coal Oil Point, two games of spikeball in the sand and groups of surfers in the water and sunset in the dappled clouds before us.
