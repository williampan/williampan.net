Finished *Titus Alone*. It differs radically from the previous two books, marking the start of a new journey that is prematurely ended by Peake's death. There's some beauty to this end: the last book shatters the certainty of Gormenghast, but does not go wide enough to mark new boundaries beyond the castle. It leaves us with a ragged, unknowable edge. This positions it even more thoroughly against *Lord of the Rings*, which is clean and contained, a world encapsulated beneath the lens of its maker.
