In the evening, I walked down Veronica Springs and into the neighborhood on the other side of Las Positas, Bel Air Knolls, up Portesuello then back along the inland segment of Crestline. The trees were few and the houses were large, the usual industrialized construction of the 70s but in strange configurations: doorways in odd shapes, roofs at steep angles. Several houses were undergoing inexplicable construction projects: palm tree saplings planted in beds of black shale, driveways paved with comically large tiles, gardens with fresh soil exposed. A house had hung a pair of American flags constructed of light-emitting diodes; another had a flag the size of a garage door. On Crestline people held parties on their back porches, facing the hills. 

Henry picked me up in that neighborhood, and we drove into Westside and circled back up Cliff and got a burrito from Super Cucas, 9m88 playing the whole way. We then went to my house and watched *Goodbye South, Goodbye* on the projector: vivid scenes of tropical idyll, patterned shirts, and unfulfilled glory.
