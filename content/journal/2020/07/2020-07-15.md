My dream started in a warehouse building, high ceilings, fluorescent lights, books scattered on racks and across the floor. Our job was to unpack and catalog them. A group of adults in uniform announced to us what we should do, and when they were leaving an Asian kid next to me asked for the cigarette and lighter back that he had given to me and someone else for safekeeping. We dropped the lighter; the supervisors came for us; I was sent to an adjacent room to mark the backs of my hands with a silver circle and some golden letters. Later, I was told that this meant I was now one of the supervisors' "favorite", and I was soon called in to work at his house, a strange Victorian fairytale building: pastel decor, brocade and laces everywhere, tasteless European teaware. There was a tiny cupola at the top that had an exterior staircase, and if you climbed it too quickly you'd accidentally step on the roof. When the dream ended we were putting up Christmas decorations in the house, my hands still marked with gold, no end to my servitude in sight.
