Drove to Montecito, parked by the Four Seasons, and walked eastward past the warmly lit tower of the Coral Casino, timing our crossing with the tide at that brief strip where the waves cover the whole width of sand. We entered that plot of highrises and condominiums at the far side and peered into the lamplit units: lavishly decorated, millions each. The rooms of the wealthy as appealing as always. We made our way through along clean stone paths, watching the light stream out of tall windows, glad and regretful that we weren't trust fund inheritors, couldn't lead these meaningless perfect lives. Then back to the beach again, back past the perilous swash.
