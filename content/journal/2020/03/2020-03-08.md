I arrived in London, took the bus to Oxford, met Henry at the bus stop, dropped my things off; we put his laundry in the dryer; got brunch; met Daniel; took a brisk tour through the Bodleian, the Gladstone Link, and the Radcliffe Camera; met Will and Michael at the Exeter Mound; returned to the apartment; left for Tea Club; drank the Nepali teas brought by a Slovenian man who runs a tea house in Prague and now teaches at Oxford; watched his presentation; joined Toshiki (Henry's friend, a Japanese diplomat to Russia) and Yoriko (Toshiki's friend, visiting for the day, who works at the Japanese embassy in Delhi, has spent time in the States, and speaks English better than some Americans); dropped off a friend at the Trinity choir rehearsal; toured University College; saw the Shelley memorial; wandered northward; ended up at the Somerville College Chapel evening service; listened to the contemporary selection of songs and readings ("Hope is the thing with feathers", an excerpt from the *Vishnu Purana*); watched a small French child run rampant in the aisles; smiled as Sungwon, the president of tea club, suddenly appeared in the audience; looked on in amusement as Sungwon and Toshiki asked for free meal tickets; ate the heavy British fare served at Somerville; ridiculed the abject state of British cuisine; saw Toshiki's pleasant modernist flat in St. Andrews; stopped for beers at a pub in the area; listened to Yoriko and Toshiki talk about their days in Tokyo; said goodbye to Sungwon; took the long walk back with the full moon glistening on the wet roads; and parted ways. It had been thirty hours since I slept properly. We had watched the skies of Oxford shift in color as we walked the whole day in its medieval streets. I had smiled more and spoken more and met more people than I had in months.
