In last night's dream, I moved from a world of modernist wooden buildings and strange play structures (a pole suspended vertically from a wire strung between towers), to a walled city built entirely from grey stone. The buildings were terraced pyramids reminiscent of Mesoamerica, each floor smaller than the one below it, and they were spaced closely together and connected by a dense network of stairs and alleyways. The tide would come in at regular intervals and flood the whole city, submerging the bottom floors entirely in brine. We made our way to the upper levels, followed by the rising water, and then went down again as the tide receded. On the bottom levels I noticed the waterproof furniture and oxygen suits of the inhabitants. 

Adjacent was a town that looked northern European, idyllic and filled with sunshine. In the gardens were ancient birds that looked like small dinosaurs, with wings folded in grotesque ways and small white blossoms growing from the tips of their feathers. We tried to take photos of them but they moved too quickly, and we ended up with images of reflected sunlight or of the blurred visages of the beautiful young inhabitants of the town. When we were leaving, we saw a creature that looked like a small anteater, again with white and yellow flowers blooming on the tips of its fur.
