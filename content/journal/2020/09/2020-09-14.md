Watched *Ju Dou* (1990). Amazing scenes: dyed cloths hanging in the sun, the varying room elevations, the clan gatherings, the funeral, the drownings, the ultimate fire, the children's song that ties it all. 

In August, I made my way through most of *The Languages of North America* by Marianne Mithun. Languages entirely foreign to the linguistic models I know of Europe and Asia. Polysynthetic languages seem numbingly difficult to learn as an adult, the pursuit of a lifetime: so much morphology and syntax to master.

Still studying Taiwanese.
