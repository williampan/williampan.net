Left around four on a drive to Simi Valley. Scouted the territory: a set of hills that overlooked the highway, across from the giant smiley that graces southbound drivers, with that dismal tract of houses on the other side of the highway with no distinctive cultural or commercial center. We parked by a gate which we later found out was the entrance [Hummingbird Nest Ranch], a venue owned by a Vietnamese American blood products billionaire, [Kieu Hoang](https://www.forbes.com/profile/kieu-hoang/#3a6fae3e17b7). Then drove through Canoga Park—down and up Topanga Canyon Blvd—and decided to return to the Hummingbird Trail, scale the boulders, see the moon. We crossed the barren gulch at the foot—what looked like a dirtbike course—eventually joining the orthodox trail and the Mexican teens and families that had gathered among the rocks. Moments of that hike reminded me of *Punishment Park*: out of breath, a dull chest pain, covered in sweat, the sand and foxtail and burclover chafing against the skin, the terrain revealing the weakness of your joints. We wandered off the trail and rested on the sandstone until the sun set, then snapped a few photos and found the trail again in the remaining glow to get back to the car. 

We then drove to Alex's house, picked him up, drove aimlessly toward San Gabriel and shouted at each other about where to get food until we ended up by the Chongqing noodle shop again, its neon sign beckoning, and resigned ourselves to our fate there. By then it was around nine. We got the food and drove to a shaved ice place nearby, ordered two bowls of layered snow, and wolfed everything down in the car.
