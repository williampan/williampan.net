* At the time of the gold rush, fewer than fifty Chinese people lived in the continental US. (26)
* Afong Moy, the first recorded Chinese woman in America, came to New York as part of a cultural exhibit. (26)
* Chang and Eng Bunker, the eponymous Siamese twins, eventually ran their own plantation in North Carolina, owned thirty-three black slaves, married two local white women, and fathered twenty-one children. (27)
* Between the 1840s and 1870s, three-quarters of a million Chinese men were sold as slaves to work in South America and the Caribbean. (30)
* The Chinese introduced the water wheel to American placer mining. (40)
* Chinese miners evaded tax collectors so effectively that the San Francisco government employed Maidu Indians to track them down. (43) 
* Chinese people were called "Celestials”, because they claimed to be part of a celestial kingdom. (46)
* The Central Pacific sent recruiters to China to find new railroad workers. (57) 
* In the winter of 1866–67, Chinese workers carved a working city under the snow. They operated by lantern light in a network of tunnels beneath the crust, with snow chimneys and snow stairs leading up to the surface. (60) 
* Years after the railroad was completed, some Chinese railroad workers journeyed back to the Sierra Nevada to search for the remains of their colleagues, eventually shipping twenty thousand pounds of bones back to China. (64)
